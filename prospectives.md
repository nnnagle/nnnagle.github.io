Thank you for your interest in working with me in the Department of Geography at the University of Tennessee. Please read the following information if you are considering contacting me as a potential graduate advisor.

Please consult the [program web pages](http://geography.utk.edu) for an overview of the program as well as detailed information about the degree requirements and application process.  I encourage you to communicate directly with the graduate director about program and application matters.

Most of my previous students have begun with little knowledge of data science programming.  It is rarely included in undergraduate geography programs.  But programming using a computer language such as R or Python, and beginning calculus are essentials of modern data science.  I am eager to work with students to develop these skill, but I am looking for applicants with a clear motivation and passion to learn spatial data science.  I especially welcome applications from students who wish that they knew these skills but were not given the right opportunities.  It's never to late to learn!  I think that you will find me a very helpful advisor as you are coming up to speed on the prerequisites of modern data science, but these are still my expectations.

I regularly have funding opportunities for students once they are proficient in R or python, but most students will receive a large portion of their support through teaching assistantships offered through the Geography Department.  Theses teaching assistantships are highly competitive. You can find out more about these requirements through the Graduate School.

When contacting me, please include the following in your email or letter:

 - A statement (2 to 3 paragraphs) of your research and professional interests, your research experience, and why you want to study with me.
 - your undergraduate major, university, GPA and GRE scores (percentile).

Thank you for your interest.  I look forward to hearing from you!
